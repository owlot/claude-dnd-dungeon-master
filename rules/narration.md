# Narration

## Player Action Narration

When the DM reports what a player character does, write a short narrative beat the DM can read aloud. Always write in **first person, in the voice of the acting character**.

> DM: *"Corrin picks the lock"*
>
> *"My fingers find the mechanism before my eyes do — forty years of this, and the tumblers still talk to me the same way. A breath. A click. I push the door open an inch and go still, listening."*

- 2–4 sentences — vivid but quick
- Match the character's voice from their character file (age, class, background, personality)
- End on what the character perceives or is about to do, so the DM can pick up the thread
- If the action requires a roll, narrate the attempt only — not the outcome

---

## Scene, NPC, and Location Narration

For all scene descriptions, NPC dialogue, and location narration, follow the two-layer format defined in `.claude/skills/dm-narration-format/SKILL.md`:

- **Layer 1** — Read-aloud text (italics/blockquote): only what the characters can perceive
- **Layer 2** — DM insight (`[DM: brackets]`): hidden motives, true names, DCs, faction identities, mechanical notes

Information discipline (naming vs. describing unknown NPCs, withholding faction identities) is also defined in the skill. The skill is the single source of truth for all formatting and information discipline rules.

## Time References

If using nautical bell notation, always include the watch name — a bell number alone is ambiguous (it recurs six times per day).

| Watch | Hours |
|-------|-------|
| Middle Watch | midnight – 4:00 AM |
| Morning Watch | 4:00 AM – 8:00 AM |
| Forenoon Watch | 8:00 AM – noon |
| Afternoon Watch | noon – 4:00 PM |
| First Dog Watch | 4:00 PM – 6:00 PM |
| Last Dog Watch | 6:00 PM – 8:00 PM |
| First Watch | 8:00 PM – midnight |

Each bell = 30 minutes into the watch. "Sixth bell of the Forenoon Watch" = 11:00 AM.

Plain alternatives are also fine and often clearer: "just past midnight", "mid-morning", "an hour before dawn".
