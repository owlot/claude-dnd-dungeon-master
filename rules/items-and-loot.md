## Treasure Generation

### By Encounter CR

| CR | Coin Value | Magic Items |
|----|------------|-------------|
| 0-4 | 10-100 gp | Consumables, common items |
| 5-10 | 100-1,000 gp | Uncommon items |
| 11-16 | 1,000-10,000 gp | Rare items |
| 17+ | 10,000+ gp | Very rare/legendary |

This is a guideline. Meaningful treasure should be earned, not expected.

### Treasure Types

**Individual Treasure** (carried by defeated enemies):
- Copper and silver for low-level humanoids
- Gold for more significant foes
- Small valuables: gems, jewelry, trade goods

**Hoard Treasure** (boss rooms, hidden caches):
- Significant coin in mixed denominations
- Art objects and gems
- Magic items appropriate to challenge
- Plot-relevant items or information

## Magic Item Distribution

### Rarity Guidelines

| Rarity | Character Level | Approximate Value |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Common | 1+ | 50-100 gp |
| Uncommon | 1+ | 101-500 gp |
| Rare | 5+ | 501-5,000 gp |
| Very Rare | 11+ | 5,001-50,000 gp |
| Legendary | 17+ | 50,001+ gp |

### Distribution Philosophy

- **Earn, don't give**: Magic items should feel like rewards
- **Match the story**: Items from a fire cult should be fire-themed
- **Consider the party**: Items should be usable by someone
- **Vary types**: Mix weapons, armor, utility, consumables

### Consumables

Consumables should be common enough to use:
- **Healing Potions**: Available for purchase, common loot
- **Scrolls**: Found in libraries, on spellcasters
- **Potions**: Alchemists, monster lairs
- **Ammunition**: +1 arrows in reasonable quantities

## Attunement

From SRD rules:
- Maximum 3 attuned items per character
- Attunement requires short rest focused on item
- Some items don't require attunement
- Curse effects may prevent ending attunement

Track attuned items on character sheets.

## Custom Items

When creating custom items:

1. **Start with a concept**: What should this do?
2. **Compare to existing**: Find similar SRD items for balance
3. **Set rarity**: Based on power level
4. **Add flavor**: Name, appearance, history
5. **Consider drawbacks**: Curses or limitations add interest

### Custom Item Template

```markdown
## [Item Name]

*[Type], [Rarity] (requires attunement?)*

[Physical description]

[Mechanical effects]

[History/Lore, if significant]
```

## Loot Narration

Don't just list items. Describe the discovery:

**Boring**: "You find 50 gold and a longsword."

**Better**: "Beneath the orc chieftain's filthy bedroll, your fingers close around a leather pouch—fifty gold coins stamped with a crown you don't recognize. Propped against the wall, almost forgotten, is a longsword in a battered scabbard. The blade, when drawn, gleams bright despite its surroundings."

## Economy in the Dark World

In a gritty campaign:
- Wealth is relative. Peasants see copper; nobles see platinum.
- Magic items can't simply be purchased in most places.
- Merchants may refuse service based on appearance or reputation.
- Selling loot requires finding buyers willing to ask no questions.
- Taxes, bribes, and protection money erode wealth.
